# Emprint Brand System
---
# Brand Voice
## Core Tone
Emprint speaks like:
- a quiet archive
- a printed memoir
- a personal notebook
- a terminal journal
- a thoughtful letter left behind
The voice should feel:
- reflective
- calm
- literary
- tactile
- personal
- restrained
- warm
- timeless
The product should never sound:
- overly corporate
- aggressively inspirational
- startup-oriented
- productivity-obsessed
- hyper-optimized
- engagement-driven
Emprint is not trying to sell attention.
It is inviting people
to leave traces of themselves.
---
## Language Style
Prefer:
- short reflective sentences
- poetic but understandable wording
- emotional clarity
- calm pacing
- thoughtful phrasing
Avoid:
- excessive exclamation
- aggressive CTAs
- hype-driven copy
- technical overload
- marketing buzzwords
---
## Preferred Vocabulary
Emprint frequently uses words related to:
- traces
- archive
- memoir
- fragments
- anthology
- shelf
- paper
- ink
- commit
- path
- memory
- preserve
- timeline
- journal
- reflection
- quiet
- curation
- permanence
---
## Words to Avoid
Avoid language associated with:
- startup culture
- creator economy
- productivity SaaS
- social media growth
Examples:
- maximize engagement
- optimize workflow
- growth hacking
- creator monetization
- scale faster
- productivity boost
- viral content
- content engine
---
## Writing Philosophy
The writing should emphasize:
- process over outcomes
- traces over achievements
- time over speed
- curation over accumulation
- permanence over trends
The product should feel
emotionally human,
not commercially optimized.
---
# UI / Art Direction
## Emotional References
Emprint visually blends:
- old newspapers
- printed books
- paper archives
- ink on textured paper
- monochrome terminal shells
- literary magazines
- local text editors
- LP record shelves
- quiet libraries
The interface should feel like:
a personal archive built inside a terminal.
---
## Visual Personality
The UI should feel:
- tactile
- slightly nostalgic
- archival
- warm
- literary
- terminal-inspired
- handcrafted
- calm
- intentional
Not futuristic.
Not flashy.
Not hyper-modern SaaS.
---
## Color Direction
Primary atmosphere:
- muted brown
- faded paper tones
- warm grayscale
- charcoal black
- dusty beige
- ink-like dark surfaces
Accent color:
- deep orange
The orange should feel:
- like aged ink
- terminal cursor glow
- printed highlights
- warm tungsten light
Avoid:
- neon saturation
- bright gradients
- glossy modern palettes
- excessive contrast
The color system should feel:
aged,
quiet,
and long-lasting.
---
## Texture & Material
Important visual references:
- paper grain
- ink spread
- newspaper texture
- printed imperfections
- terminal phosphor glow
- worn bookshelves
- analog materials
Subtle texture is encouraged.
Perfectly sterile UI is discouraged.
---
## Typography Direction
Typography is central to Emprint.
The interface should prioritize:
- readability
- rhythm
- whitespace
- literary pacing
- printed composition
Typography should feel:
half terminal,
half printed publication.
Avoid:
- oversized startup hero typography
- overly geometric modern fonts
- playful social UI typography
---
## Layout Philosophy
Layouts should feel:
- spacious
- composed
- calm
- editorial
- shelf-like
- archival
Avoid:
- dense dashboards
- infinite feed structures
- metric-heavy UI
- noisy cards
- addictive scrolling patterns
Scrolling should feel intentional,
not compulsive.
---
## Interaction Philosophy
Interactions should feel:
- quiet
- deliberate
- tactile
- slow enough to notice
Animations should be subtle and restrained.
The product should reward:
reflection,
not stimulation.
---
# Information Architecture
## Landing Page Philosophy
The landing page should communicate:
why Emprint exists,
before explaining features.
Emotional understanding
is more important than technical understanding.
Users should first feel:
- "this is a space for me"
- "this feels personal"
- "this feels lasting"
before learning implementation details.
---
# Recommended Landing Structure
## 1. Hero Section
Goal:
Introduce emotional philosophy immediately.
Should include:
- main slogan
- emotional identity
- quiet visual composition
Example themes:
- process over results
- personal traces
- your own space
- leave your path behind
Avoid:
- feature dumping
- technical explanations
- productivity framing
---
## 2. Why Emprint Exists
Explain:
- modern platforms prioritize outcomes
- people are more than polished results
- developers already have commit history
- non-developers deserve equivalent archives
This section establishes the philosophical problem.
---
## 3. Commit Philosophy
Introduce:
- publishing as commits
- visible timelines
- growth through revisions
- accumulated traces
This is one of Emprint's defining concepts.
---
## 4. Anthologies
Explain anthology types emotionally,
not only functionally.
Anthologies should feel like:
different shelves inside a personal archive.
Important:
focus on meaning and mood.
---
## 5. Fragments
Present Fragments as:
a curated visual shelf.
Do NOT frame limitations
as storage constraints.
Instead frame them as:
intentional preservation and curation.
---
## 6. Blank
Introduce Blank as:
the unrestricted anthology.
It should communicate:
creative freedom,
code-driven expression,
and handcrafted web identity.
Blank is where:
terminal culture and personal publishing merge.
---
## 7. Quiet Archive Philosophy
Clearly explain:
Emprint is not social media.
No algorithmic pressure.
No engagement chasing.
No endless feed consumption.
Emprint values:
quiet permanence.
---
## 8. Closing Section
The ending should feel:
reflective,
warm,
and lasting.
Users should leave with the feeling:
> "This could become my place."
Avoid:
aggressive signup prompts
or startup-style urgency.
---
# Design Constraints for AI Systems
When generating content or UI for Emprint:
ALWAYS prioritize:
- emotional clarity
- calmness
- literary tone
- permanence
- tactile atmosphere
- curated pacing
NEVER default to:
- startup SaaS aesthetics
- creator economy framing
- social media UX patterns
- engagement optimization
- hyper-technical branding
Emprint is a personal archive,
not a content machine.
